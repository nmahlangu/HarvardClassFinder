[ FAQ ]

What is Theharvardclassfinder?

  • It is an elegant solution to the problem of finding classes to take at Harvard. It allows you to rank classes by course number, overall Q score, difficulty, workload, and peer recommendations.


How do I use Theharvardclassfinder?

  • It's simple, just select an option from each drop down menu on the home page and then click search!


Is this data official?

  • Yes! All the data you see being displayed on this website is taken directly from the Harvard Q.

Will my HUID and pin be stored when I login?

  • No! In fact, the database never even has access to it. The entire login process is handled by Harvard's own CS50 ID service and is in place only to ensure that nonauthorized people (e.g. Yale students) aren't able to access data from the Harvard Q.


A specific class I'm looking for is not showing up. Are all of Harvard's classes in the database?

  • Yes and no. While all of the classes exist in the database, results are filtered so that the data you are presented with is the most helpful. For example, classes that are not currently available to take or don't have data yet (e.g. new classes), are factored out and not shown.

Why Theharvardclassfinder?

  • Looking at the competition, the Harvard Q is extremely slow, doesn't show you information for more than one class at a time, and is almost painful to use. CS50 Courses is better but shows you a lot of classes that have blank or "TDB" fields, and also doesn't allow you to use a ranking scheme besides overall Q score when browsing classes.

Will there be future updates?

  • Yes! New features and the most recent data will always be added to the site in future updates. This is version 2.0
